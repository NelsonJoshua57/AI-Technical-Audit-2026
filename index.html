Phantom impressions

Impressions spiked but CTR collapsed to 2.26%. AI reads your page, extracts the answer — your site never gets the visit.

<200ms is the new KPI

Can an AI agent crawl, reach, and extract a fact from your deepest page in under 200 milliseconds? If no — everything else is irrelevant.

04 / 09
Bot deep dive

What user bots
actually do

AI user bots = your real opportunity

These are triggered when a real person asks ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Claude a question and the AI researches the answer on their behalf. The only visits that translate to actual AI visibility.

The 3-click cliff

AI search bots drop off sharply beyond 3 clicks from the homepage and typically visit each deep page only once a month. Pages beyond 4 clicks are effectively invisible.

Training ≠ Visibility

Your site can receive heavy crawling from training bots and still be completely absent from AI-generated answers. These are fundamentally different bot types with different impacts.

Perplexity-User exception

Perplexity-User (the user-triggered bot) does NOT respect robots.txt — confirmed by Cloudflare. PerplexityBot does. Build strategy with this asymmetry in mind.

Opportunity unlocked

Fan-out queries &
phantom impressions

What is fan-out?

AI agents decompose a single user prompt into dozens of parallel sub-queries. 95% of queries driving AI citations have zero monthly search volume — they're synthetic, never appeared in any keyword tool.

Query types that fan out most

List queries, comparison queries, and product review intents fan out most heavily. "Product review" intent queries grew 16,000% in just 3 months in 2025 — AI agents harvesting structured opinion data.

How to find fan-out opportunities

In GSC Performance, filter for:
Query length >7 words
Impressions <50
Clicks = 0
Last 3 months

Note: GSC UI only shows 1,000 rows. Use GSC API or export in date chunks, then merge in BigQuery or Sheets.

Don't filter these out

If you're ignoring long-query, zero-click impressions in your reporting, you're flying blind. These are real signals that your content is inside AI reasoning chains.

Action plan · Steps 1–3

The 5-step audit:
where to start

1

Segment AI bot traffic in server logs

Pull Apache/Nginx logs and filter for: OAI-SearchBot, ChatGPT-User, PerplexityBot, Perplexity-User, Claude-SearchBot, Claude-User. Separate training bots from user bots. Pages user bots visit = scale; pages training-only = fix first.

2

Audit technical accessibility of deep pages

For a sample of important deep URLs: view raw HTML to confirm content is not JS-injected, count click depth from homepage (<4 required), test load time in DevTools (target <200ms), and check for accordions or "View More" hiding key content.

3

Audit and clean your robots.txt

Review every Disallow/Allow directive line by line. Ensure you're not blocking AI crawlers you want in. Remember: Perplexity-User ignores robots.txt regardless. A 30-minute audit here has outsized impact.

08 / 09
Action plan · Steps 4–5

Complete the
audit loop

4

Map your phantom impressions & fan-out queries

Export GSC Performance data filtered for: 7+ word queries, <50 impressions, zero clicks, last 3 months. This is your Fan-Out Opportunity Matrix — the exact questions AI agents are asking about your content. Use the GSC API to bypass the 1,000-row UI limit. Exclude long queries appearing daily (likely human). Then create content structured as lists, comparisons, and reviews to capture this surface area.

5

Set up ongoing monthly monitoring

Pull GSC data monthly and compare impression trends for long queries. Re-run log analysis and diff AI bot activity month-over-month by bot type. Track Core Web Vitals separately in PageSpeed Insights or CrUX. A drop in AI user bot visits to a page cluster is your earliest warning signal.

Priority start: Audit your robots.txt today. Then segment AI bot traffic in logs. Everything else follows from those two actions.
